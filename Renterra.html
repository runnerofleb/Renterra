Target Account 01 — Hillview Equipment Highest Priority — Mock Call Target96/100

Business snapshot: Directional profile indicates established heavy-equipment rental focus with transport, service, and contractor account complexity.

BDR Point of View

Best first call candidate due to operational density and likely revenue lift from tighter quote-to-dispatch execution.

Likely Operational Pain

Quote follow-up inconsistency, limited fleet visibility by location, inspection documentation fragmentation, and month-end billing compression.

Renterra Value Map

Inventory/availability + dispatch board + inspection capture + invoicing automation unlock faster turns and cleaner cash collection.

Discovery Questions

  • How do you confirm true availability before promising delivery?
  • What percent of quotes receive a follow-up within 24 hours?
  • How are inspections tied back to contracts today?

Opening Wedge

“When heavy equipment sits one extra day due to coordination gaps, utilization drops fast. Worth comparing your current dispatch/quote workflow against what top independents are doing?”

Cold-Call Framework

Opener — permission & pattern interrupt

“Hi {{Name}}, Chris with Renterra—caught you for 30 seconds? I noticed you’re balancing rental + service activity across {{branch/market}} and I have one question on how your team handles quote follow-up.”

Observation — specific research callout

Reference branch footprint, contractor-heavy demand, or dealer/rental overlap to show relevance.

Problem question — ask, then shut up

“When quotes go out, what process ensures nobody drops follow-up by end of day?”

Value bridge — map Renterra to confirmed pain

Tie exact pain to module: follow-up automation, live inventory, dispatch board, inspections, billing.

Close — ask for 20 minutes, not a sale

“If we can show a cleaner way to raise utilization and reduce billing chaos, open to a 20-minute working session next week?”

Close Script

“Not asking for a platform change today. Just a practical comparison call with your current workflow. If it’s not better, you’ll know quickly. Tuesday or Thursday better?”

Objection Handling

  • We’re all set: “Makes sense—usually means no urgent fire. Which area is least painful today: quotes, dispatch, or billing?”
  • We already use Point of Rental / Texada: “Helpful context. Teams call us when they need faster adoption and easier daily execution.”
  • Just send me an email: “Happy to. What should I include so it’s actually relevant to your operation?”

More Responses

  • We’re too small: “That’s exactly where small inefficiencies hit hardest. We focus on operator time recovered.”
  • How much does it cost? “Depends on branch/fleet complexity; can I first scope where you’d capture ROI?”
  • I’m too busy: “Understood—would a focused 12-minute compare on Friday morning be easier?”

Game-Day Execution Plan — Hillview Mock Call

Run-of-Show

Cold open: Permission-based opener, contractor context, one problem question.
Listening for: quote SLA, availability confidence, dispatch latency, inspection proof gaps, billing lag.
AE handoff framing: “Prospect confirmed dispatch/quote follow-up pain and interest in utilization lift; demo should focus on branch inventory, follow-up automation, and billing close-out.”

Do / Don’t

  • Do lead with operational credibility.
  • Do quantify time/revenue leakage.
  • Don’t pitch all modules at once.
  • Don’t ask for a sale on first call.

Day-of Checklist & Success Criteria

  • Verify contact role and branch scope.
  • Prepare one-sentence POV + two research callouts.
  • Secure scheduled 20-minute discovery with stakeholders.
  • Capture one measurable pain metric to carry into AE call.
