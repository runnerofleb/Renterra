Strategic thesis

The issue is not demand. It is execution.

These companies already serve contractors. The opportunity is helping them move faster and reduce manual work across the rental process.

Renterra wins when a rental shop has enough demand, but the day-to-day workflow is still held together by phone calls, spreadsheets, texts, memory, and manual follow-up.

Cold-call framework

Five simple moves to earn the next call.

The goal of the first call is not to sell the product. It is to get them talking, confirm one real pain point, and earn a short meeting with an AE.

1

Opener

Name the cold call, ask for 30 seconds, and make it easy for them to say yes or no.

2

Observation

Use one specific detail about their business so they know the call is not random.

3

Problem question

Ask one clear question about how they run today. Then pause and listen.

4

Value bridge

Repeat their pain in plain English, then connect it to one Renterra capability.

5

Close

Ask for 20 minutes with an AE. Keep it low pressure and specific.

Hey [Name], this is Chris from Renterra. I’ll be honest — this is a cold call. Mind if I take 30 seconds to explain why I’m calling, and then you can tell me if it is relevant?
I’m not trying to sell you on this call. I’d like to set up 20 minutes where an AE can show what this looks like for a shop like yours. If it is not useful, no harm done. Fair?
Pain mapping & objections

Keep the conversation practical.

Every response should stay grounded in their workflow: quotes, inventory, dispatch, inspections, billing, or follow-up.

“We’re still on paper and spreadsheets.”
One system for inventory, dispatch, and follow-up
“Quotes go out and we forget about them.”
Automated quote follow-up by text and email
“I don’t know what’s idle vs. what’s earning.”
Utilization and revenue-per-asset visibility
“Customers keep asking to book online.”
Branded storefront and customer portal
“Billing is a mess at month-end.”
Automated invoicing, payments, and aging balances
“We’re on Point of Rental but it’s clunky.”
Modern UI, mobile-first workflow, lighter implementation
“Inspection photos live on someone’s phone.”
Inspection photos tied directly to the rental contract
“We’re all set / not interested.”

Totally fair. Before I let you go, what is the biggest time-suck in the office right now? If it is not something we can help with, I will leave you alone.

“We already use Point of Rental / Texada.”

That makes sense. If you were starting from scratch today, would you choose the same setup again? I’m mostly curious where the current system still creates friction.

“Just send me an email.”

Happy to. So I send the right thing, which area should I focus on — quotes, inventory, dispatch, inspections, or billing?

“We’re too small for software.”

That is usually when this starts to matter: small teams with enough asset volume that paper, spreadsheets, and manual follow-up start slowing things down.

“How much does it cost?”

It depends on locations and asset count, so I do not want to guess. A short AE call would give you the workflow and a real number based on your setup.

“I’m too busy right now.”

I hear you. Quick one — are you the right person for rental operations software, or is there someone else who owns that?

Game-day execution

Hillview mock-call run-of-show.

Lead with Hillview because the fit is easiest to explain: heavy equipment, high-value assets, transport, service, parts, and rental workflow complexity.

I noticed Hillview rents, sells, transports, and services heavy equipment across New England. Usually at that point, the hard part is keeping machine availability, inspection condition, transport timing, and service status aligned. How are you managing that today?

Listen for

  • Manual availability checks
  • Inspection photos outside the rental workflow
  • Transport handled by text or whiteboard
  • Quote follow-up handled by memory
  • Service status not visible to the right people

Do

  • Use the prospect’s name naturally
  • Match their pace
  • Let silence breathe
  • Take notes they can hear
  • Be honest if you do not know something

Don’t

  • Pitch features too early
  • Use corporate jargon
  • Fill every silence
  • Guess on pricing
  • End without a clear next step

Day-of checklist

  • Hillview account card open
  • Blank physical notepad and pen
  • Quiet room and strong audio
  • Water nearby
  • Two specific time slots ready for the close
