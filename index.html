Cold-call framework

Five moves to earn a committed next step.

The goal of the first call is to take control quickly, earn attention with a relevant business reason, uncover one real operational issue, and push for a clear next step.

1

Pattern interrupt

Open directly, sound confident, and get permission for a quick reason-based conversation.

2

Reason to call

Lead with one specific detail from their business: fleet, branches, transport, service, or quote volume.

3

Operational question

Ask where the current process slows down, costs time, or creates customer friction.

4

Business impact

Tie the answer to speed, missed revenue, asset utilization, service delays, or payment follow-up.

5

Clear ask

Ask for a specific meeting time and push for a real commitment while the pain is fresh.

Cold opener

Hey [Name], this is Chris with Renterra. I saw you rent and service heavy equipment, and I’m calling because teams like yours can lose a lot of time keeping quotes, machine status, transport, and service updates lined up. Quick question — how are you tracking that today?

Commitment close

Based on what you said, this is exactly the kind of workflow Renterra helps clean up. Let’s put 20 minutes on the calendar with one of our AEs so you can see what this would look like for your shop. Are you better Tuesday morning or Wednesday afternoon?

Pain mapping & objections

Keep the conversation grounded in workflow.

Every response should stay grounded in their workflow: quotes, inventory, dispatch, inspections, billing, or follow-up.

What I hearRenterra angle / response
“We’re still on paper and spreadsheets.”One system for inventory, dispatch, and follow-up.
“Quotes go out and we forget about them.”Automated quote follow-up by text and email.
“I don’t know what’s idle vs. what’s earning.”Utilization and revenue-per-asset visibility.
“Inspection photos live on someone’s phone.”Inspection photos tied directly to the rental contract.
“We already use Point of Rental / Texada.”That makes sense. If you were starting from scratch today, would you choose the same setup again?
“Just send me an email.”Happy to. So I send the right thing, should I focus on quotes, inventory, dispatch, inspections, or billing?
Game-day execution

Hillview mock-call plan.

Lead with Hillview because the fit is easiest to explain: heavy equipment, high-value assets, transport, service, parts, and rental workflow complexity.

Primary prompt

I noticed Hillview rents, sells, transports, and services heavy equipment across New England. When a business has rentals, transport, inspections, service, and parts all touching the same machines, status accuracy becomes expensive fast. How are you managing that today?

Listen for

  • Manual availability checks
  • Inspection photos outside the rental workflow
  • Transport handled by text or whiteboard
  • Quote follow-up handled by memory
  • Service status not visible to the right people

Do

  • Use the prospect’s name naturally
  • Lead with the reason for the call
  • Match their pace
  • Let silence breathe
  • Take notes they can hear
  • Be honest if I do not know something

Don’t

  • Pitch features before confirming pain
  • Sound too soft or overly scripted
  • Fill every silence
  • Guess on pricing
  • End without asking for a specific meeting time
