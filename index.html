Research thesis

Renterra wins where the bottleneck is execution: fast quotes, clean inventory status, dispatch coordination, service readiness, inspection records, payments, and follow-up discipline.

Target accounts

Hillview Equipment96/100 · Highest priority

Primary mock-call target with strong rental operational complexity.

Open strategy details

Business snapshot: Multi-line equipment rental operations with dispatch and turn pressure.

Likely pain: Delays between quote, availability checks, and ready-to-rent status.

Renterra value map: Unified quote-to-contract flow + inspection/service visibility + payment follow-through.

Discovery questions: Where do deals stall most often? How is branch inventory truth maintained? Who owns missed follow-up?

Opening wedge: “If I could help your team shorten quote-to-confirm without adding headcount, worth a quick compare?”

Equipment East94/100 · Strategic priority

Regional footprint with branch coordination challenges.

Open strategy details

Business snapshot: High transaction velocity across locations.

Likely pain: Fragmented updates between sales, dispatch, and service.

Renterra value map: Shared operating system for quoting, dispatch, and billing handoffs.

Discovery questions: How often do promised delivery times slip? Where is status currently tracked?

Opening wedge: “Can I share what similar teams changed to stop order-status blind spots?”

Game-day execution plan

Hillview mock-call run-of-show

  • Cold open with permission + relevance anchor.
  • Confirm one bottleneck with targeted question.
  • Bridge to Renterra workflow outcomes.
  • Secure 20-minute discovery with right stakeholders.

Operator checklist

  • Listen for: manual steps, branch disconnects, follow-up gaps.
  • Do: mirror language, quantify impact, confirm next step owner.
  • Don’t: over-demo, feature dump, negotiate price too early.
  • AE handoff: summarize pain, urgency, economic stake, buying team.
  • Success criteria: meeting booked with contextual agenda.
